Core metadata: creator

The creator of a resource is the person or organization who generated the resource, rather than the party who puts the resource on-line; c.f. publisher in the core metadata. (The creator and the publisher might well be the same party.)

It is usually more helpful to nominate an organization as creator rather than an individual with an organization; organizations are more permanent. However, individuals may be named if this gives credit more accurately.

The creator name is free text, e.g. CASU or Cambridge Astronomical Survey Unit. The creator IVO identifier is a URI pointing to a separate registration of the creator as an organization; e.g. ivo://uk.ac.cam.ast/CASU. The name is mandatory and the IVO identifier is optional. Note that the IVO identifier has to be a URI in the ivo:// scheme; a URL in the http:// pointing to a web-page is not correct.

A URL to the creator's logo may also be published. This shows up as a "brand" in certain user interfaces.
