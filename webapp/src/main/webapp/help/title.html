Core metadata: title

The title describes the registered resource as a whole. It is typically used as a label in lists of similar resources. Therefore, it is best to pick a title that makes sense in a general context. SDSS DR5 object catalogue is better than SDSS catalogue because it may appear in a list of several SDSS releases. SDSS alone fails to distinguish different kinds of VO resource (e.g. image and catalogue services) build from SDSS data. Object catalogue would be a very poor title; you cannot assume that the viewer knows from context the survey to which it applies.

Titles can be of any length, but long titles will cause problems when displayed in lists. Therefore, keep your title as short as possible; 50 characters is a practical limit and 30 characters is better.
