Core metadata: publisher

The publisher of a resource is the person or organization who put the resource on-line. It does not refer to the original creator of the resource (c.f. creator in the core metadata).

It is usually more helpful to nominate an organization as publisher rather than an individual with an organization; organizations are more permanent. You can nominate a contact person within the organization separately (see the contact fields of the core metadata.

The publisher name is free text, e.g. CASU or Cambridge Astronomical Survey Unit. The publisher IVO identifier is a URI pointing to a separate registration of the publisher as an organization; e.g. ivo://uk.ac.cam.ast/CASU. The name is mandatory and the IVO identifier is optional. Note that the IVO identifier has to be a URI in the ibo:// scheme; a URL in the http:// pointing to a web-page is not correct.

When you set up the registry the self-registration process may have created an organization registration for you.
